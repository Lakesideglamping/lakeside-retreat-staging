Whether you're here to explore or simply unwind, every day at Lakeside Retreat offers something special.

  • Walk through the vineyard at golden hour sunrise and sunset are equally stunning
  • Soak in your private spa, then cool off with a swim in Lake Dunstan
  • Spot swans, eagles, and native birds nesting around the vineyard
  • Fire up the BBQ on your deck with vineyard views
  • Stargaze from your dome the Milky Way is breathtaking here
  • Watch the sunrise from bed through floor-to-ceiling windows
  • Explore award-winning wineries and cellar doors minutes away

  • Cycle the Lake Dunstan Trail E-bikes available nearby
  • Hike local tracks with stunning lake and mountain views
  • Go-kart at Highlands Motorsport Park

Beautiful in Every Season

Central Otago transforms with the seasons each one offers a unique experience

Summer (Dec - Feb)

Swim in Lake Dunstan, cycle the trail in long golden evenings, and enjoy alfresco dining at the region's best restaurants. Peak season book early.

Autumn (Mar - May)

Our favourite season. The vineyard turns gold, harvest brings winery events, and the mild days are perfect for exploring. Stunning photography opportunities.

Winter (Jun - Aug)

Frosty mornings, hot tub under the stars, and five ski fields within reach. Cosy up in your dome with mountain views and a glass of local Pinot Noir. Pure magic.

Spring (Sep - Nov)

Blossoms, lambs, and fewer crowds. Great shoulder-season rates and the best weather for hiking. Central Otago's hidden gem season.

Central Otago Rail Trail Guide

Your complete guide to New Zealand's most famous cycle trail, starting just 300m from your accommodation

From Your Accommodation

Access Point: 300m walk to Cromwell section
Trail Surface: Sealed and gravel, suitable for all fitness levels
Bike Storage: Secure storage at property
We Provide: Trail maps, tire pump, basic tools
Bike Hire: Cromwell (10min drive) or Clyde (15min)

Most Popular Sections

1. Cromwell to Clyde (15km) - Easy, 1-2 hours
Lake views, historic gold mining sites, cafs in Clyde

2. Clyde to Alexandra (33km) - Moderate, 2-4 hours
Classic rail trail experience, Poolburn Gorge

3. Alexandra to Omakau (20km) - Easy-moderate, 1-3 hours
Rural farmland, historic railway stations

Essential Items

Water: 2+ litres for longer sections
Sun Protection: Hat, sunglasses, sunscreen (high UV)
Snacks: Energy bars, lunch for longer rides
Layers: Weather changes quickly in Central Otago
Phone: Download offline maps, emergency contact
Cash: Some trail cafs are cash-only

From Wrong Turn to Dream Come True

April 2019 One Accidental Discovery A Lifetime of Lakeside Living

Stephen and Sandy took the wrong turn on Smiths Way in 2019. Three hours later, sitting by Lake Dunstan surrounded by Pinot Noir vines, they knew they'd found something extraordinary. Five years later, their "wrong turn" has become Central Otago's most loved luxury retreat.

"We sat on that lake shore for three hours, just talking about what if. What if we could wake up to this view every day? What if we could share this incredible place with others? What if that wrong turn was actually the right turn all along?"
Stephen & Sandy, Your Hosts

Stephen & Sandy

Your Hosts at Lakeside Retreat

The Wrong Turn That Changed Everything

It was April 2019, and we were utterly exhausted. Sandy was working in Wellington and Stephen had spent years in retail. We desperately needed a weekend away from the city, so we loaded up the car and headed for Central Otago, thinking we'd find a nice hotel and do some wine tasting.

That's when we took the wrong turn on Smiths Way.

Instead of reaching our planned accommodation, we found ourselves pulling over at what can only be described as the most beautiful lakefront vineyard we'd ever seen. Five hectares of established Pinot Noir vines rolling down to the shores of Lake Dunstan, with the Remarkables rising majestically in the distance. There was a small 'For Sale' sign barely visible amongst the vines.

"We sat on that lake shore for three hours, just talking about what if. What if we could wake up to this view every day? What if we could create something genuinely sustainable here using our technical backgrounds?"

Taking the Leap

Six months later, we owned 96 Smiths Way. It wasn't a rational decision by any measure. Sandy continued her work remotely, whilst Stephen left retail to focus on transforming this stunning vineyard into something extraordinary.

We found the perfect solution for the vineyard: leasing it to experienced local wine growers who understand this terroir. This allows the vines to continue producing exceptional fruit under expert care, whilst we focus entirely on creating unforgettable experiences for our guests.

Sustainability was always a personal mission, not just marketing. We invested in a solar system that actually produces more energy than the retreat uses but our real focus was on making every guest feel like they've discovered somewhere truly special.

Building Something Special

Making the retreat fully sustainable was our biggest challenge and greatest achievement. We wanted guests to enjoy five-star comfort whilst knowing their stay was actually giving back to the environment and after months of planning, that's exactly what we built.

"The day we realised we were producing more energy than we use and feeding the surplus back into New Zealand's grid was incredibly satisfying."

Our first guest review mentioned the 'rustic charm' of having no hot water. We learned quickly that good intentions need proper backup plans and that YouTube University only gets you so far! We've come a long way since then, with battery storage ensuring uninterrupted comfort in every season.

Three Unique Stays, One Stunning Location

Each accommodation tells a different story. Stephen designed Dome Pinot as the flagship experience the views from the bed encompass the entire lake and surrounding mountains. Those panoramic windows took weeks to get right, but waking up to that view makes it all worthwhile.

Dome Rose became our romantic retreat. Sandy's idea to include the saltwater spa proved inspired watching couples relax whilst enjoying sunset over Central Otago never gets old.

The Lakeside Cottage was Sandy's solution for families and groups. Direct lake access, complete cooking facilities with outdoor BBQ, and space for six people. The private spa overlooking the vineyard has become the most photographed spot on the property.

Why Guests Love It Here

Every guest who chooses Lakeside Retreat becomes part of our story. You're experiencing Central Otago from one of its most magical vantage points, supporting sustainable tourism, and enjoying the kind of personal hospitality that only comes from hosts who genuinely love what they do.

Stephen has found his calling in hospitality his favourite part of each day is helping guests discover the best wineries, hidden cycle trails, and local gems that only locals know about. Sandy keeps everything running smoothly behind the scenes, and together they make sure every detail is taken care of.

"We're not just offering accommodation we're sharing our corner of Central Otago with you. Every guest becomes part of our story."

Today and Tomorrow

Five years later, we're still pinching ourselves. We wake up to Lake Dunstan at our doorstep every morning. The vineyard continues to produce beautiful Pinot Noir grapes that our wine-growing partners transform into exceptional bottles. And the retreat produces more energy than it uses something we're quietly very proud of.

Most importantly, we've learned that luxury and sustainability aren't contradictory they're complementary. Our guests consistently tell us that the combination of stunning views, genuine hospitality, and knowing their stay is eco-friendly makes for the perfect escape.

When you stay with us, you're not just booking accommodation. You're supporting local wine growers who understand this special terroir. You're experiencing Central Otago from one of its most magical vantage points. And you're becoming part of our story one that started with a wrong turn and led to the best decision we ever made.

Come and be part of our story.

Stephen & Sandy

Your Hosts & Founders, Lakeside Retreat

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I check in?

Check-in is from 3pm. We'll send you a detailed arrival guide with directions, gate code, and welcome instructions 2 days before your stay. Stephen or Sandy will greet you personally on arrival to show you around the property and answer any questions.

What's your cancellation policy?

Full refund if cancelled 7+ days before check-in. 50% refund for cancellations 3-7 days before. No refund within 3 days of check-in. The $300 security bond is a pre-authorisation only and is released 48 hours after checkout.

How do I get to Lakeside Retreat?

From Queenstown Airport: 55 min drive via SH6. Head towards Cromwell, turn onto Smiths Way off SH8B.
From Dunedin Airport: 2.5 hours via SH8 through the stunning Maniototo.
Car hire: We recommend hiring a car as Central Otago is best explored at your own pace. All major rental companies operate from Queenstown Airport.

What should I bring?

We provide: Towels, robes, toiletries, linen, kitchen essentials, BBQ, wine glasses, coffee & tea.
You should bring: Warm layers (Central Otago can be cool even in summer evenings), sunscreen, swimwear, walking shoes, and your favourite wine from a local cellar door visit!
Dome guests: Breakfast provisions are stocked for you. Cottage guests have full cooking facilities.

Can I bring my pet?

The Lakeside Cottage is pet-friendly with prior approval ($25/pet/night cleaning fee, max 2 pets). Please let us know breed and size when booking. The domes are not suitable for pets. All pets must be well-behaved and supervised at all times.

What's the best time to visit?

Summer (Dec-Feb): Lake swimming, cycling, long warm evenings. Peak season.
Autumn (Mar-May): Stunning golden vineyard colours, wine harvest season, mild days. Our favourite season.
Winter (Jun-Aug): Frosty mornings, hot tub under the stars, 5 ski fields nearby. Cosy and magical.
Spring (Sep-Nov): Blossoms, lambs, fewer crowds, great deals. Central Otago's hidden gem season.

Why book direct instead of Airbnb?

Booking direct saves you money Airbnb and Booking.com add 12-18% in service fees. When you book with us directly, you get the same great stay at a better price. You also get direct communication with your hosts and more flexibility with special requests.
